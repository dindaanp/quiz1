Tulungagung

Tulungagung is like a hidden gem in East Java, how could it not be? This city offers stunning natural charm with a variety of amazing tourist destinations. From exotic beaches with soft white sand to towering green mountains. Tulungagung has everything to satisfy the adventurous soul. Enjoy the beauty of the natural panorama that is still natural, fresh air, and the friendliness of the local people.

3 tourist places in Tulungagung

Pacar Beach

Pacar Beach, Tulungagung. Pacar Beach is one of the tourist destinations in Tulungagung, East Java. Pacar Beach is located in Pucanglaban Village, Pucanglaban District and is flanked by two other beaches, namely Molang Beach and Lumbung Beach. One of the attractions of Pacar Beach is its calm and natural atmosphere. This destination has not been visited by many tourists. Why is this beach called Pacar Beach? Some people may not know that the word "pacar" in the name of the beach does not refer to the term special friend who has a special relationship. It is said that Pacar Beach was originally over grown with plants and trees. One of the plants that dominates this area is the water pacar plant. This is a shrub with beautiful, colorful flowers.

Ranu Gumbolo

Ranu Gumbolo is a lake with water flowing from the Wonorejo Reservoir in Tulungagung. So the lake here can be said to be part of the reservoir. Even so, the atmosphere and views around it are able to spoil the eyes. Because of its beauty, this tourist spot has now become a leading tourist spot in Tulungagung. The name Ranu Gumbolo Tulungagung itself comes from Ranu Kumbolo or the famous lake on the Mount Semeru climbing route. At first glance, the appearance of Ranu Gumbolo Tulungagung is similar to Lake Ranu Gumbolo. So, it is named similar to Ranu Gumbolo.

JLS

The Tulungagung Southern Cross Route (JLS) is a road that connects the beaches in the south of East Java. This JLS has many advantages. JLS Tulungagung offers views of the expanse of blue sea, green hills, and rows of beaches with white sand. Around JLS there are also various places to enjoy culinary delights, such as grilled fish at Klatak Beach, seafood at Prigi Beach, and traditional snacks along the road. JLS Tulungagung holds various historical and cultural relics, such as the Gayatri Temple, a relic of the Kadiri Kingdom and the "Sedekah Laut" tradition in Besuki Village. The soothing beach breeze and various interesting photo spots make JLS Tulungagung a leading tourist spot in Tulungagung.
